== Introduction to visual impairment and assistive technologies for independent living

=== Visual Impairment

Many types of visual impairments with varying levels of severity

Italian law (law 138) guarantees that people with visual impairments can live life with some adaptations

* they provide some definitions as well
** total blindness
** partial blindness
** severe/moderate/slight impairment

Decrees relating to access to education have been in the legal system since the 1920s (i.e. during the monarchy)

* there used to be special schools/institutions until the 1970s
* in 1976 people with VI were allowed to acces public schools

=== Touch and hearing

People with VI rely more on touch and hearing to perceive the world

* braille
* street tracks
* screen readers

==== Braille code

Grid with 6 dots

* encodes stem:[2^6] characters (64)
** informatics braille uses stem:[2^8] (256)

Braille is written with:

* slate
* pointy implement

Stab the slate in the corresponding pattern using a guide grid

There are also braille typewriters

* they emboss braille into paper sheets
