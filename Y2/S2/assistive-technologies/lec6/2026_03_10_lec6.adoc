== Deafness

Hearing deficit

* partial
** difficulty hearing
** deaf in one ear but not the other
* total
** can't hear at all in either ear

Not always immediately detectable

* can't always tell if someone is deaf
** often only apparent when interactions occur
* easier to tell if someone is blind

Deafness also affects production of language

* spoken or not
** cochlear implants make speaking weird
** not everyone knows sign language

Interaction is very difficult

* can cause isolation
* stereotypes and poor technology exacerbates this (big words today)

== Communication

3 broad categories of communication styles:

. oralists
* do not use sign language
* rely on lip reading

. bimodals
* lip reading
** using local words
* supported by signs

. signers
* rely on sign language only
** non deaf children with deaf parents
** deaf children during adolescence
** interpreters

Generally complex sentences/words are difficult to parse for deaf people

* non deaf people learn by listening
** usually listening first
* deaf people cannot learn by listening
** complex ideas need to be translated into simpler ideas
** makes reading more difficult as well
** can't read it out in their mind easily

=== Technological support

* Assistive listening devices
** simply to enable hearing
** works on partially deaf people

* Communication apps
** apps made specifically for deaf people

* Transcription/dictation
** speech to text

* Subtitles/closed captions/live transcription
** especially for video calls

These are for spoken communication

* Need to hear environment as well
** doorbells
** alarms
** ambient sounds

==== Limitations

Most technologies are built for general daily life content

* single language
* generic content

Specialized communications are often not considered:

* math
* computer science
* learning other languages

Special software is required

* ComMunico (EU commission)
* IntendiMe
** IoT haptics

=== Sign language

* varies country by country

Difficult to understand if untrained

300 sign languages worldwide

* 45 countries recognize sign languages in Europe

Recognizing it as a language -> improves integration

=== Speech

Speaking louder doesn't help

* especially if totally deaf

Cochlear implants can help in some scenarios

== Hearing assistance

=== Cochlear implants

Electronic device that enables hearing

* not as if they were not deaf but at least they can perceive sound
* auditory signals are sent straight to the nerves
** bypasses damaged areas of the ear
* usable since childhood
** at least 1 year of age

Difficult to install

* medical device
* specialist intervention required

Very useful

* can interact with external devices
** depends on specific brand/model/whatever

=== Hearing aids

Simpler than cochlear implants

* amplifies sounds
* transmits through normal audotiry channels

== Deafblindness

Combination of deafness and blindness

* severe communication and mobility deficit
** worse than being deaf or blind

Basically makes all deafness workarounds worthless

* sign language doesn't work
* lip reading doesn't work
* subtitles/captions/live transcriptions don't work either
* audio transcriptions don't work

Haptics are really the only way

* Braille
* Malossi
